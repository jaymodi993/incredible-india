Tamil Nadu

Experience the Ancient Heritage

A fascinating destination in south India, Tamil Nadu, bears imprints of ancient Dravidian culture dating back to over two millennia. Its diverse landscape; mapped with golden beaches, lush paddy fields, ancient cities, and charming hill retreats; is as alluring as its ancient temples, and enduring culture that still lives in its many traditions and art forms.

Home to delicious Idli-Sambhar, gorgeous Kanchipuram Sarees, divine Carnatic music, and spellbinding Bharatnatyam dance; Tamil Nadu is a dream destination that promises you a remarkable experience.

Tamil Nadu Special

Temples of Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu’s rich legacy of temple architecture is quite extraordinary and perhaps unparalleled in the world. Exquisitely carved and rainbow-hued, these rock-cut temples are marvels of Indian architecture. Meenakshi Amman temple in Madurai, Brihadeswara temple in Tanjore, Kapaleeshwarar temple in Chennai, and Ramanathaswamy temple in Rameswaram are some of the finest temples of Tamil Nadu.

Alluring Destinations of Tamil Nadu

Chennai

Chennai, the quaint capital of Tamil Nadu, is the custodian of its ancient heritage and distinct culture. A city of temples, beaches, churches, malls, and glitzy amusement parks; Chennai is a bundle of spiritual & cultural charms that blend seamlessly with its colonial legacy & modern chic.

Coimbatore

Coimbatore is the second largest city of Tamil Nadu after Chennai. It lies in a beautiful location on the bank of the Noyyal River, ringed by the Western Ghats. The city packs in a lot of scenic beauty with lakes and waterfalls.

Kodaikanal

There is something hypnotizing about the scenery of Kodaikanal that is evocative of jade greenery, sparkling lakes and thunderous waterfalls. The name of the hill-station, Kodaikanal is a Tamil word which means 'The Gift of the Forest'.

Madurai

The colorful & exquisitely sculpted Gopurams (entrance towers) of Meenakshi Amman Temple dominate the skyline of Madurai. The city, almost as old as the Dravidian civilization and suffused with the fragrance of incense & jasmine flowers, is a beautiful answer to your spiritual quest.

Trichy

Tiruchirapalli, fondly called Trichy, is the fourth largest city of Tamil Nadu state. Its history dates back to 3rd century BC when it was under the reign of Chola dynasty. Several other dynasties including Pallavas, Pandyas and Vijaynagar, invaded and lent their contribution in its beautification which makes it one of the top attractions in the country.

Mahabalipuram

Spectacular shore temples, exquisitely carved caves, massive bas reliefs, and stunning monoliths greet you in this beautiful hamlet by the sea. A UNESCO-listed heritage site, Mahabalipuram depicts the exceptional craftsmanship of Tamil artisans.

Kanchipuram

This ‘golden city’ of Tamil Nadu is home to many magnificent temples that have preserved the faith for centuries. The city, once an education center for both Jains & Buddhists, is also known for its gorgeous Silk saris woven by local weavers.

Rameshwaram

Shrouded in veils of mythology, this beautiful island is not only one of the holy Char-Dhams of Hindu pilgrimage but also a place of untouched beauty.

Kanyakumari

An appealing combination of cerulean waters, vermillion washed sunsets and tranquility; Kanyakumari is a perfect vacation spot on the southernmost tip of Tamil Nadu. Its magical allure of the mythical land’s end, the confluence of three seas and the virgin goddess, adds to its mystical charms.

Ooty

Ooty’s mist-swathed mountains beckon you away from the grind of city life. Ride the century-old heritage toy train that chugs up steep mountains through bridges and tunnels; ride a boat in the lake and walk through the scenic tea plantations for a delightful Ooty experience.
